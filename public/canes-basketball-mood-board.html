Creative Overview

Five Directions on the Table

These are five distinct ways the June shoot could go. Each has a different feel, a different production style, and would result in a different final deliverable.

They live on a spectrum from most raw (Style 1) to most polished (Style 4), with Style 5 as a hybrid wildcard. The shoot doesn't have to be 100% one style — realistic blends are at the bottom.

Where we're leaning: Style 1 (Raw BTS Documentary Compilation) is the direction the master reference doc is currently built around. This page exists so that lean can be validated, not assumed.

Style 01

Raw BTS
Documentary
Compilation

"The Players' Tribune mini-doc approach"

A chronicled day in the life of an athlete moving through the Cane's production day — arrival, studio, car ride, court, refuel. Handheld. Natural light. No posing. The athlete feels real, not packaged. The texture of effort: sweat, breath, focus, the moments between the hero shots.

Best For
Long-form social compilation cuts. Brand storytelling that feels authentic, not transactional. Letting athlete personality drive the content.
Tradeoffs
Less iconic single-frame content for OOH or standees. Requires strong editing to feel cohesive across 43 athletes.
Final Cut
A 60-second TikTok/IG Reel: athlete arrives at studio, brief studio moments, car ride conversation, court workout, refuel with Cane's. Music carries it. No narration.

Style 02

Cinematic
Hero Showcase

"The sneaker commercial approach"

Each athlete is treated like the subject of their own short film. Slow-motion. Dramatic angles. Rich color grading. The court action is framed to look as cinematic as possible. Less "athlete in their element," more "athlete as iconic figure."

Best For
Hero stills for OOH, billboards, in-restaurant standees. TV-ready CTV spots. Iconic single-frame moments that scale across mediums.
Tradeoffs
Loses the authentic feel currently working in Cane's content. More time per athlete (4–6/day vs 8–9). Could read as "another sneaker ad."
Final Cut
A 30-second CTV spot: dramatic intro, slow-mo workout sequences, hero portrait moments, ends on Cane's logo. Polished, commercial, network-ready.
Style 03

Vertical-First
Social Native

"Built for the algorithm"

Every frame conceived for vertical (9:16) first. Fast cuts. On-screen captions and graphics. Designed for TikTok / IG Reels native feel — the kind of content that doesn't look out of place on someone's For You Page. Authentic feel, but stylized for platform engagement.

Best For
IG Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts as primary delivery. Algorithm-friendly content that doesn't feel like an ad. Younger audience engagement.
Tradeoffs
Hard to repurpose for horizontal CTV or OOH. Trends shift quickly — content can feel dated within months.
Final Cut
A 30-second IG Reel per athlete: hook in 2 seconds, fast cuts of training/refuel/studio, on-screen captions, trending audio. Designed to live in feed.

Style 04

Editorial
Portrait

"The SLAM magazine cover story approach"

Magazine-quality stills as the hero deliverable. Deliberate composition, controlled lighting, formal weight to every frame. Less video-forward, more photo-essay. Each athlete is captured with dignity and intentionality, framed like a portrait subject.

Best For
Print campaigns, magazine ads, in-restaurant signage. "Hall of fame" feel for standees. Building Cane's as a brand that takes its talent seriously.
Tradeoffs
Heaviest production day (lighting, art direction, time per athlete). Less social-native content out of the same shoot.
Final Cut
A photo essay published on Cane's social and partner channels: 5–7 stills per athlete, each frame-worthy. Supporting 30-second video. Feels like a magazine spread.
Style 05

UGC × Doc
Hybrid

"The athletes filmed parts of it themselves"

Mixes traditional camera coverage with footage that looks like the athlete filmed it on their phone. Voice memos, talking-head moments, vlog energy. Feels intentionally low-fi in spots, polished in others. The brand is in the room, but it doesn't feel like it's directing the room.

Best For
Authenticity-first marketing. Building athlete-as-collaborator relationship. Content that doesn't feel like content. Multi-platform delivery.
Tradeoffs
Hardest to control quality/consistency across 43 athletes. Requires athletes to actively participate — some will, some won't.
Final Cut
A 90-second cut that opens with the athlete filming themselves on the way to the shoot, transitions into traditional coverage, returns to phone-style for the refuel. Feels like a friend documented their day.

Strategic Options

Realistic Blends

01 + 03

Raw BTS captured, then edited vertical-first for social. Likely the strongest combo for a Cane's NIL campaign in 2026. You get the authentic documentary footage, and the edit team cuts it for each platform natively.

01 + 04

Documentary video + editorial stills. Best of both worlds for multi-platform delivery. The BTS doc runs on social; the portrait stills run on OOH, standees, and in-restaurant.

01 + 05

Documentary footage with athlete-generated inserts. Ask athletes to film 30 seconds of phone content on the way in. Weave it into the doc. Maximum authenticity with professional coverage as the backbone.

02 + 04

Cinematic court footage + editorial stills. If Cane's wants something that scales to TV and print. Heavier production load, but every frame is gallery-quality. Best for a campaign with OOH ambitions.
