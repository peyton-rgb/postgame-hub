Direction · Documentary
The Thesis

Not a commercial.
Documentation.

Small camera. Available light. The brand in the environment, not staged into it. We're shooting the moments between the moments.

The Approach

One day, one camera,
one athlete.

A videographer shadows each athlete through their full day — from the Cane's studio shoot, through the drive to the court, and into practice. We cut a vlog-style day-in-the-life for long-form and pull a stack of vertical clips from the same footage for Reels and TikTok.

Reference clips throughout come from last season's football Cane's shoot — different sport, same approach. This upcoming shoot is basketball.

Location 02 · The Drive

Studio to court.

One sustained clip. Athlete on camera in the passenger seat. The driver becomes the interviewer — a short list of questions, answered in real time. This is where the day-in-the-life finds its voice.

The Drive
Athlete on Camera
Driver mic'd up off-camera. Athlete in the passenger seat, talking through the day on the way to the court.
Sample questions for the driver
  1. How did today feel different from a normal day on the court?
  2. What was the weirdest part of being on a real set?
  3. If you could shoot one more take, what would you change?
  4. What does your pre-game ritual look like — and did the studio mess with it?
  5. Cane's day. Be honest — what's your order?
The Venue

Vanta court.

Rooftop sports facility on top of Plaza of the Americas in downtown Dallas — minutes from the studio. Same day, different setting: open sky and the city skyline as the backdrop. The shift from controlled studio black-box to wide-open urban rooftop is the visual hook of the back half of the day.

Location 03 · Court

Back in their element.

Workout day. Athlete out of talent mode, back in their world — drills, shootaround, the grind. Then Cane's lands and the day winds down with the meal that fuels the whole shoot.

Beat 01 — Arrival
Gearing Up
Bag drop, lacing up, taping. The shift from talent back to athlete. Close on hands and feet.
Beat 02 — The Work
Getting Loose
Warmups, shootaround, individual drills. Finding the rhythm before the work picks up.
Beat 03 — Going Live
Full Speed
Scrimmage, full-court runs, the grind. Sweat, breath, between-rep beats. Long lens from the baseline.
Beat 04 — The Refuel
Cane's Lands
Boxes hit the sideline. Athlete eats, recovers, decompresses. The meal that fuels the whole shoot — and the vlog's natural close.
