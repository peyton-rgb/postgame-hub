Scene 02 — The 3-Point Shootout

Lethal Shooter
vs. the House

The 3-point shootout: Red Bull's own shooting savant against the school's stars.

Think the pregame kicking contest that eats College GameDay every Saturday — the spectacle the whole crowd drifts toward, films, and argues about. Ours is a 3-point shootout: Matthews — Red Bull's own, trainer to NBA and WNBA stars — takes the arc against the school's signed shooters. Best of 25, crowd counts every make, winner takes the belt for the campus. A promotable, repeatable main event — announced all week, clipped all weekend.

The Trophy

Won at center court, worn all season, defended at the next stop — the Red Bull chain travels with whoever holds it. Lethal Shooter arrives in Baton Rouge wearing it. Twelve campuses get a shot to take it off his neck.

The 12-Week Slate — Follow the Football

Twelve Saturdays. The biggest game each week — and the hooper who owns that campus.

The court follows the sport's center of gravity: each week's marquee football game sets the stop, and the host school's basketball star headlines the 3-point shootout against Lethal Shooter. Five stops are covered by athletes already signed with Postgame — the rest we contract with Red Bull, campus by campus. And every stop carries an alum card: if the current star can't headline, the school's basketball legends can — retired icons and WNBA stars are in their offseason on fall Saturdays, and September stops fall inside the NBA offseason window.
