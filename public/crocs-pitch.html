§ 01 The partnership

Four years.
Built on repetition.

What's been built

The relationship started in 2022 with a bet: give Crocs to nearly 1,000 college athletes in a single four-week window, ask each one to post, and see what happens when authentic content hits Gen-Z at scale. It became one of the most impactful campaigns Postgame has ever run.

Since then, the model has kept working. Postgame tapped its network of 70,000 college athletes to deliver a repeatable mix of large-scale gifting, high-profile elevated content shoots produced by a nationwide videographer network, and physical retail activations with Dick's Sporting Goods in Knoxville and Champaign.

What's on this page is the shape of that relationship in 2026 — organized into the three program types that make Postgame × Crocs work: reactive moments caught in real time, pro athletes who carry the brand from college into the league, and in-person events that turn the network into foot traffic.

§ 02 · Reactive

React to the
moment.

The entire reactive model is built for this: when a Crocs athlete makes news — a buzzer-beater, a Sweet 16 run, a draft night moment — Postgame's network is already on campus, the creative is already built, and the content is in-feed the next day. No quarterly brief. No scramble. Just speed.

Jaden Bradley hit a buzzer-beater to win the Big 12.
His Crocs content was live the next day.

Arizona's Big 12 Player of the Year didn't just carry his team to its first Final Four in 25 years — he gave Crocs one of the cleanest reactivity case studies of the entire tournament. Postgame's team was already holding the creative. When the moment happened, we shipped.
